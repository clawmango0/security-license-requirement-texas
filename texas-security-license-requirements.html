🔒 Texas Security License Requirements

Applicant Profile: 20 years old with NREMT EMT Basic certification

⚠️ Important: The Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS) regulates private security licenses. Requirements can change — always verify current requirements directly with Texas DPS at dps.texas.gov or call their office.

📋 Overview

In Texas, security professionals are regulated by the Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS) Regulatory Services Division. There are two main types of licenses:

  • Commissioned Security Officer (requires firearm authorization)
  • Non-Commissioned Security Officer (no firearm authority)
Source: Texas Department of Public Safety - Regulatory Services Division

📝 General Requirements (All Applicants)

Requirement Details Source

⚠️ Age Requirement Alert: At 20 years old, you do NOT meet the minimum age requirement for a Commissioned Security Officer license in Texas, which requires applicants to be 21 years or older.

🔫 Commissioned Security Officer (Level II)

Requires:

  • Must be 21 years of age or older
  • Complete 40-hour Level II Training Course (Classroom + Range)
  • Firearms qualification (revolver and semi-automatic)
  • Pass state examination
  • Submit to fingerprint-based criminal background check
  • No disqualifying criminal convictions
  • No history of mental illness or substance abuse
Source: Texas Occupations Code §1702 | Texas DPS Private Security Bureau

🛡️ Non-Commissioned Security Officer (Level III)

Requires:

  • Must be 18 years of age or older
  • Complete 20-hour Level III Training Course
  • Pass state examination
  • Submit to fingerprint-based criminal background check

This option IS available to you at age 20.

Source: Texas Occupations Code §1702 | Texas DPS Private Security Bureau

🏥 Your EMT-Basic Certification

💡 Valuable Addition: Your NREMT EMT-Basic certification is a significant plus for security work, especially in:
  • Healthcare facility security
  • Event security with medical standby
  • Industrial sites requiring medical response capability
  • Executive protection with medical considerations
  • Emergency response roles

While it doesn't exempt you from any licensing requirements, it makes you a more valuable candidate and may qualify you for higher-paying positions or specialized roles.

Source: National Registry of Emergency Medical Technicians (NREMT)

📌 Recommended Path at Age 20

  1. Start with Level III (Non-Commissioned) License - Available at age 18+
  2. Gain experience in the security field
  3. Upgrade to Level II (Commissioned) when you turn 21
